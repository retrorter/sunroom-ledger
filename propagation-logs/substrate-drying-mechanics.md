### 🏠 The Indoor vs. Outdoor Thermodynamic Leap

Managing coco hydration indoors is notoriously tedious. Without active solar radiation or radiant ground heat, you are relying entirely on ambient evaporation and localized airflow. Manually skim-removing the dry top layer and constantly shifting the damp mass underneath is the only way to prevent the core from souring indoors, but it turns a simple task into a multi-day chore. 

Pivoting this exact technique to the driveway under a 100°F+ sky is going to feel like a massive upgrade. The manual redistribution habits you developed indoors will make you even more effective out there, but the **evaporation rate will increase exponentially**. Instead of waiting days, you'll see massive plumes of moisture hitting the plastic envelope within minutes of spreading it out.

---

### ⏳ The Industrial Longevity of Dry Coir

It is an engineering testament to compressed coconut coir that blocks purchased in 2024 behave identically in 2026. 

Because the material is thoroughly washed, graded, and dehydrated down to a minimal internal moisture coefficient before mechanical compression, it enters a state of complete biological arrest. As long as they are stored away from ambient ambient humidity or liquid leaks, those lignified fibers won't break down, oxidize, or lose structural integrity. 

You are essentially waking up a perfectly preserved 2-year-old skeleton mix. Once that hot water hits them in the buckets, they will perform exactly like a freshly pressed batch.
