### 🪵 Turmeric & Ginger Co-Habitation Protocol

Yes, you can absolutely place the turmeric rhizomes directly into the same incubation container with the ginger for the pre-sprout phase. 

Because both belong to the *Zingiberaceae* family, their structural mechanics during early activation are identical. Resting them horizontally on top of that **1/2-inch layer of damp perlite** at a steady **77°F** is the textbook way to wake up those dormant eyes. 

### Rhizome Pre-Sprouting :: Propagation Technique
An open, shallow incubation setup on non-organic media allows for maximum oxygenation around the rhizome casing. This minimizes the risk of fungal or anaerobic rot during the weeks it takes for the vascular roots to drop, long before the plant can process bulk soil moisture.

*Note for later down the road:* While co-habitating for the sprout phase is perfect, they will eventually need separate setups or a very wide container when potted into soil. Ginger rhizomes expand horizontally right along the subsurface line, whereas turmeric pushes deeper and forms a denser, vertical cluster that requires total soil disruption to harvest.
