### 🔍 Root Status Calibration

Understood—the starter roots are already live and deployed from the transition protocol. Having those root tips present changes the math. Even though the roots are active, the mother leaves (*Kalanchoe tomentosa*) still possess massive internal moisture and nutrient reserves. 

The leaf with the brown tissue producing the finest, most delicate roots is your highest-risk profile. Those ultra-fine root hairs are highly susceptible to immediate cell wall collapse if they face an aggressive moisture load, but they can also desiccate if left completely dry for too long once they actively begin searching for a resource.

---

### 🎯 The Primary Hydration Triggers

Do not follow a calendar schedule. Instead, wait for these two specific physiological triggers to occur simultaneously before introducing the first drop of water:

*   **Trigger 1: Substrate Anchorage (Mechanical Resistance)**
    *   The roots must grow long enough to actively travel downward into your perlite and chunky matrix. 
    *   **The Test:** When you give the leaf a microscopic, gentle nudge, you should feel slight mechanical resistance. If the leaf slides freely across the top layer, the roots are still purely atmospheric and cannot absorb substrate moisture. Watering now will only trigger stem rot.
*   **Trigger 2: Mother Leaf Turgor Deficit (Signs of Thirst)**
    *   Look for slight wrinkling, puckering, or a minor loss of rigidity on the main body of the mother leaf. 
    *   As long as the parent leaves remain plump, smooth, and rigid, they are successfully plumbing water from their own internal parenchymal tissue to feed the root tips. External water is mechanically redundant until this internal reservoir begins to deplete.

---

### 🛠️ First-Watering Execution Protocol

When both triggers are met, your first moisture application must match the scale of the root systems:

*   **No Bulk Soaking:** Avoid pouring water across the whole pot. The total volume of your chunky mix cannot be processed by micro-roots, creating an immediate stagnant zone.
*   **Hyper-Localized Drizzle:** Use a precise tool (like a syringe, pipet, or narrow squeeze bottle) to apply a few milliliters of water **strictly at the base** where the roots enter the grit. 
*   **Dry-Down Velocity:** The moisture should evaporate completely from that top layer within 24 hours. This micro-dose encourages the fine roots on that brown-tipped leaf to branch out further into the substrate in search of the next pocket of humidity, without suffocating them.
