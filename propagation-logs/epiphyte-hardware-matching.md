### 🔬 The Mechanics of Terracotta Vents

Pairing your chunky **#4 perlite** mix with a **terracotta orchid pot** creates a highly efficient passive evaporative system:

*   **Unglazed Clay Porosity:** Unlike plastic or glazed ceramic, raw terracotta acts as a breathable, semi-permeable membrane. It wicks excess water laterally out of the substrate profile and allows it to evaporate through the pot walls, lowering the risk of a soggy core.
*   **Side Ventilation Slits:** The cutouts in the sides completely break up the internal boundary layers. Instead of air only entering from the top surface of the soil, atmospheric oxygen penetrates the root zone from a 36xl0° geometry. 
*   **Thermal Regulation:** As moisture evaporates through the clay walls, it naturally cools the root zone. For a stressed epiphyte recovering in a warm sunroom, this replication of cool, breezy canopy conditions reduces metabolic respiratory stress.
