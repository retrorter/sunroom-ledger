### 🌿 Axillary vs. Auxiliary (The Botanical Distinction)

As a programmer, think of this as a namespace collision between anatomy and system architecture. They sound nearly identical when spoken, but they map to completely different concepts:

### Axillary (with an "i")
*   **Definition:** Relating to the **axil**—the precise upper angle/junction where a leaf petiole or side branch meets the main stem. It comes from the Latin *axilla*, meaning armpit.
*   **Context:** This is the *only* correct term for plant nodes. An **axillary bud** is the dormant cellular cluster sitting right in that structural "armpit" (like the nodal eyes on your *Ficus Audrey* props or *Kalanchoe* stems). 

### Auxiliary (with a "u" — often misspelled as "auxillary")
*   **Definition:** Providing supplementary, secondary, or backup support. 
*   **Context:** This is a functional description, not an anatomical location. You use an **auxiliary** Lasko fan to boost airflow, or an **auxiliary** light fixture to increase your daily light integral (DLI). 

> 💡 **The Quick Compilation Check:** If you are talking about a physical location on a plant stem, it is always **Axillary**. If you are talking about helper hardware or a secondary system, it is **Auxiliary**.
