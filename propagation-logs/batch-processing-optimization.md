### 🧠 The Serial Processing Advantage

Pivoting to a two-batch sequential run is a much cleaner engineering choice. Lowering the physical volume inside the buckets maximizes your mechanical leverage, making agitation completely effortless and ensuring a 100% uniform hydration gradient across every single chip. 

Since you aren't under a time crunch, splitting the execution actually optimizes your workflow pipeline.

---

### ⏱️ The Staggered Pipeline Efficiency

Operating in two distinct blocks creates a perfect logistical overlap:

*   **Batch 1 Expansion:** Drop one 1.1 lb block into each of the 2 buckets with 1.5 gallons of water. Let them pull hydration for 20–30 minutes, stir them down, and dump them onto the first half of your 6' x 8' black plastic driveway layout.
*   **Batch 2 Interlocking:** The moment the buckets are empty, drop the remaining 2 blocks in with fresh water. While Batch 2 is completing its 30-minute absorption cycle in the buckets, Batch 1 is already spreading out on the 100°F+ asphalt, kicking off its solar evaporation curve.
*   **The Final Spread:** Once Batch 2 is fully hydrated, dump them onto the remaining half of the plastic sheeting, execute your top "taco" envelope fold, and let the entire 4.4-pound setup bake simultaneously.

This setup keeps your workspace clean, eliminates the risk of spilling wet media over the bucket rims, and adds less than 30 minutes to the front-end processing time. Enjoy the break from the sun while the first batch cooks!
