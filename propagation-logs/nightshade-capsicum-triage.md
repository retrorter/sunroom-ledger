### 🌾 Substrate Mystery Solved: Rice Hulls

The top-dress material on your pepper that resembles elongated cumin or fenugreek seeds is **parboiled rice hulls**. 

In high-drainage horticulture, rice hulls are deployed as a sustainable, silica-rich alternative to perlite or as a functional mulch layer. Mechanically, they serve three excellent purposes here:
*   **Erosion Control:** They break the kinetic energy of water drops during top-watering, preventing the fine aggregate from shifting or cratering.
*   **Moisture Conservation:** They create a shallow boundary layer that reduces surface evaporation while the core substrate dries down.
*   **Pest Mitigation:** A dry layer of rice hulls creates an abrasive barrier that disrupts the life cycle of fungus gnats.

### 🌿 Rosemary Structural Triage

The stark split in your rosemary is perfectly captured in `dessicated-rosemary-healthier-stems.jpg` and `closeup-woody-plant-and-healthy-stems.jpg`. You have two entirely different vascular realities sharing one terracotta pot.

```
                  [Rosemary Root Crown]
                           │
         ┌─────────────────┴─────────────────┐
         ▼                                   ▼
[Dead Woody Trunk]                  [Vibrant Green Canes]
  - Brittle, gray cambium             - Active xylem/phloem flow
  - WILL NOT backbud                  - Accelerating vegetative mass
  - Action: Prune to base             - Action: Conserve and trace
```

*   **Will the woody piece produce vegetative growth?** No. Rosemary lacks the aggressive adventitious budding capabilities of soft-wood herbs. Once a major woody structural branch completely desiccates, drops its needles, and turns that characteristic brittle, weathered gray, the internal vascular cambium layer is dead. It will not backbud.
*   **Pruning Recommendation:** **Yes, absolutely remove the dried-up juvenile side branches and the entire dead woody structure.** Take your shears down as close to the root crown as possible without nicking the healthy green stems. Removing this dead wood eliminates a physical vector for fungal pathogens and opens up valuable physical real estate for the healthy, trailing green branches to fold back over the center of the pot.
*   **Sunroom Props Status:** Your two cuttings in FoxFarm Ocean Forest + standard perlite are sitting in a highly nutrient-dense, moisture-retentive launchpad. Rosemary roots slowly but securely. If they've been in there for a few weeks without showing tip-wilt or desiccation, their fine, white root networks are actively mapping out the perlite voids. Leave them un-disturbed until you see root tips peeking through the bottom drainage holes.

### 🧪 Basil Linear Migration Strategy

Your canopy management in `basil-pruned-a-couple-days-ago.jpg` is text-book. Those clean axial cuts have successfully triggered opposite lateral branching, priming the plants for a bushy, multi-headed growth profile. 

Moving these specimens out of the deep, circular pot and into a linear, rectangular self-watering tomato planter matching your arugula blueprint is a stellar system upgrade for two reasons:

*   **Photon Uniformity:** A linear layout under a dual T5 fixture ensures an identical $PAR$ value across every single node. In a deep round pot, peripheral stems are forced to phototropically bend outward, leading to uneven elongation and micro-shadowing within the core canopy.
*   **Hydraulic Equilibrium:** Basil is a heavy transpiring machine that requires high volume but stable moisture. A self-watering rectangular planter with a fresh screen liner distributes water evenly along a horizontal plane, preventing the localized anaerobic "dead zones" that can occur in the deep bottom center of large round pots. 

Upgrading to an additional light rack to support this dedicated dual T5 basil run will pay immediate dividends in clean, high-velocity leaf production.
